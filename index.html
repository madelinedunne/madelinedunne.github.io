About

I am a PhD student and an ASHA-certified speech-language pathologist in the Speech, Language, and Hearing Sciences program at Boston University. I work in the Center for Brain Recovery, where my research interests center on advancing naturalistic and functional treatment approaches for aphasia, with a particular interest in conversation-based and group interventions.

Currently, I am working on a Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI) Eugene Washington Engagement Award project with the National Aphasia Association (NAA). This project, titled "Building capacity for patient-centered CER in stroke-related aphasia," aims to establish a clear set of research priorities based on the experiences and needs of people living with aphasia, ensuring that patient voices are at the center of research that affects their lives.

I am dedicated to understanding how aphasia impacts social participation and everyday functioning, and I aim to investigate outcomes that extend beyond core language impairment, including behavioral and neural markers of recovery. Informed by my clinical experience, I hope to support the translation of research into meaningful, evidence-based interventions that improve quality of life for individuals with aphasia and their families.

Research Interests

  • Functional treatment for aphasia
  • Social participation and quality of life outcomes for aphasia
  • Behavioral and neural mechanisms of treatment-induced recovery
  • Translational clinical research
