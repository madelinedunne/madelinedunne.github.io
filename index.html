Madeline Dunne

PhD Student at Boston University
Center for Brain Recovery

I am a PhD student and an ASHA-certified speech-language pathologist in the Speech, Language, and Hearing Sciences program at Boston University. I work with Dr. Swathi Kiran in the Center for Brain Recovery, where my research interests center on advancing naturalistic and functional treatment approaches for aphasia, with a particular interest in conversation-based and group interventions.

Currently, I am working on a Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI) Eugene Washington Engagement Award project with the National Aphasia Association (NAA). This project, titled "Building capacity for patient-centered CER in stroke-related aphasia," aims to establish a clear set of research priorities based on the experiences and needs of people living with aphasia, ensuring that patient voices are at the center of research that affects their lives.

Broadly, my research focuses on understanding how aphasia impacts social participation and everyday functioning, investigating outcomes that extend beyond core language impairment, including behavioral and neural markers of recovery. Informed by my clinical experience, I hope to support the translation of research into meaningful, evidence-based interventions that improve quality of life for individuals with aphasia and their families. My work spans behavioral, physiological, and neuroimaging measures, as well as patient-centered approaches to research.

Before my doctoral training, I was a speech-language pathologist in acute care, LTAC, and outpatient settings. I earned my M.S. in Speech-Language Pathology from Boston University and my B.A. in Speech, Language, and Hearing Sciences (with minors in Cognitive Neuroscience and Theater, and an ASL Certificate) from Temple University.

When I'm not working, I enjoy performing arts, crocheting, baking, reading, and game nights with friends and family.
